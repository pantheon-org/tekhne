# ScholarEval Evaluation Framework

Detailed criteria, rubrics, and quality indicators for each ScholarEval dimension.

## 1. Problem Formulation & Research Questions

**Strong (4–5):**
- Research question is specific, measurable, and bounded in scope
- Theoretical or practical significance is explicitly stated
- Hypothesis or objective is falsifiable and testable

**Adequate (3):**
- Research question is present but could be more precise
- Significance implied rather than argued

**Weak (1–2):**
- Research question is absent, vague, or overly broad
- No articulation of why this question matters

**Critical issues (score 1):**
- No discernible research question
- Question is trivially answered or unanswerable

## 2. Literature Review

**Strong (4–5):**
- Covers foundational and recent (≤5 years) literature
- Synthesises themes, contradictions, and research gaps
- Critically evaluates sources rather than summarising

**Adequate (3):**
- Coverage of major works but gaps in recent literature
- Describes sources without deep synthesis

**Weak (1–2):**
- Missing major foundational works
- Chronological listing with no synthesis

## 3. Methodology & Research Design

**Strong (4–5):**
- Design clearly matches research questions
- Validation procedures stated and justified
- Limitations explicitly acknowledged
- Reproducible: code, data, or protocol available

**Adequate (3):**
- Design appropriate but validation incomplete
- Limitations mentioned briefly

**Weak (1–2):**
- Design mismatched to research questions
- No validation or reproducibility information

## 4. Data Collection & Sources

**Strong (4–5):**
- Data source clearly described and justified
- Sample size appropriate with power analysis or justification
- Preprocessing steps fully documented

**Weak (1–2):**
- Data source unclear or inappropriate
- No justification for sample size

## 5. Analysis Rigor

**Strong (4–5):**
- Statistical tests appropriate to data type and distribution
- Effect sizes and confidence intervals reported
- Multiple comparison corrections applied where needed
- Assumptions explicitly tested

**Weak (1–2):**
- Inappropriate tests for data type
- p-values reported without effect sizes or CI
- Assumptions untested

## 6. Presentation & Scholarly Writing

**Strong (4–5):**
- Logical structure with clear transitions
- Figures and tables are self-contained with informative captions
- Conclusions follow directly from results
- Appropriate academic register throughout

**Weak (1–2):**
- Disorganised structure
- Conclusions overclaim beyond the data

## 7. Contribution & Significance

**Strong (4–5):**
- Clear articulation of what is new relative to prior work
- Practical or theoretical implications stated
- Limitations of generalisability acknowledged

## Scoring Scale Reference

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 5 | Excellent | Exemplary; publishable in top venues |
| 4 | Good | Strong with minor improvements needed |
| 3 | Adequate | Acceptable with notable areas for improvement |
| 2 | Needs improvement | Significant revisions required |
| 1 | Poor | Fundamental issues requiring major revision |
